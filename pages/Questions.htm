Formalism vs. Imitationalism: Questions

1. How might you argue that Karl Blossfeldt's photographs are abstract?

2. Find an example of architectural ornamentation that might have had its creative inspiration in the world of plants.

3. Blossfeldt's Bio: 

    a) Where was he born?

    b) Where did he study?

    c) Where did he teach?

4. In your own words, defend the following statement: Paul Strand was one of the major forces of photographic modernism.

5. Paul Strand had influences from a variety of different artists. Name the artists from each of the following disciplines that had some impact on his work:

    a) photography;

    b) painting;

    c) sculpture;

    d) film.

6. In what way is Strand's Church photograph from 1944 a formalist image?

7. What two events precipitated Weston's break with Pictorialism?

8. Weston wrote, "the camera should be used for a recording of life, for rendering the very substance and quintessence of the thing itself, whether it be polished steel or palpitating flesh."

    a) What does this mean?

    b) What evidence can you find in Weston's work to suggest he was successful in this endeavour?

9. Weston has written that photography should be "direct, honest, uncompromising". What technical tricks did he believe should be avoided by a good photographer?

10. In what way were Friedlander's Guggenheim Fellowships of 1960 and 1962 indicative of his tendency towards imitationalist aesthetics?

11. a) In what way is Lee Friedlander's Factory Valleys No. 13, 1980 a documentary?

    b) How does Factory Valleys No. 13 support a formalist aesthetic? 

12. In what way is Lange's work imitationalist?

13. How did Lange earn her living as a photographer?

14. What message is conveyed in Lange's Migrant Mother, 1936?

15. According to Newman, "The portrait is a form of biography." It documents a history that is subject to the interpretation of the photographer. How is this interpretation evident in the following portraits:

    a) President Lyndon B. Johnson, 1963;

    b) Willie (The Lion) Smith, 1960;

    c) Woody Allen, 1996.

16. There are a number of different processes that a photographer might follow to take pictures of people. In what ways does the process followed by Arnold Newman differ from that of Dorthea Lange?

17. How has Gary Winogrand's choice of equipment affected his photographic imagery?

18. What aspects of Winogrand's studies have contributed to his creative ability?

19. Winogrand's images are narratives — they tell a story. In your own words, explain the narrative from any one of the Winogrand images presented.
